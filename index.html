Don't wait for tomorrow's tech - make it.

We aim to improve AI literacy among underrepresented UK youth through free hackathons. Our events include educational seminars, project building, and extra resources.

Hackathons

Hackathons are really exciting events where you and some teammates work together to brainstorm ideas and for some, bring them to life. You can attend on your own as you are encouraged to meet new people and form new relationships over the course of the hackathon. However, feel free to come with a friend- it can seem intimidating at first so knowing someone makes the experience much easier.

You will essentially spend the day brainstorming different ideas and creating projects that solve real problems.

Our hackathons include a technical and non-technical track. Those who have previous coding experience may wish to take part in the technical track where they work to bring the idea to reality. Teams can also choose the non-technical track if they have no coding experience where they have to brainstorm an idea and create different items to present their work (e.g. an essay, a presentation, a one-pager).

Educational seminars

We start each hackathon by teaching you different concepts, either technical or based around a problem that AI systems face. The prompt for the hackathon will be based on the information from the seminar, so it’s a paramount part of the day that you won’t want to miss!

Extra resources

Ultimately, our goal is to increase AI literacy and unfortunately, we cannot thoroughly do that in just one day. That is why we will provide access to extra resources you can research in your free time. These might give you ideas for the next hackathon, or they can blossom into ideas of your own! Whatever it may be, we strongly recommend that you take a look at them.

Eligibility requirements for hackathon participants

Ages 11-18 years old

You must be between these ages to compete in the hackathon. If you would like to take part in helping this organisation’s aim, you can become a volunteer to help out at the hackathons or develop resources. Please contact us at youthinai@gmail.com.

UK resident

You must be a UK resident to take part in this hackathon. Many of the hackathons will happen in-person in the UK.

Underrepresented

  • Ethnic minorities - BAME minorities/ Roma and Traveller communities
  • Females
  • People with disabilities - If your disability is physical please contact us before the event so we can organise access arrangements to the venue
  • LGBTQ+ community
  • Neurodiverse individuals
  • Young carers
  • Indices of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) – If your home postcode is within an area that is ranked in the bottom 40% of their region by the IMD.

Responsible AI: A Policy Approach to Bias Eradication

As part of our aim to increase AI literacy among the youth we held “Responsible AI: A Policy Approach to Bias Eradication”, an interactive workshop under the AI Fringe (The AI Fringe is an organisation that aims to increase AI safety by bringing together diverse voices.) where we taught participants about the importance of safe AI. In this interactive session, featuring guest speaker Matheace Ramaputra, an AI Legal and Ethic Practitioner, we introduced the effects of AI biases on society, and potential ways to combat them through policy. Opening with a short presentation on AI biases and how it manifests in different sectors, our workshop was followed by an activity where groups will propose their own policies to mitigate them. There were many questions and open discussions.

Truth vs Tech: Tackling Misinformation and Deepfakes

At the AI and Society Forum, we spoke at the AI and Society Forum on the panel “Truth vs Tech: Tackling Misinformation and Deepfakes”. The event held over 240 people and it was uplifting seeing so many people interested in our talk, despite being the youngest speakers and attendees of the event. Throughout the day, we heard talks from many people experienced in the field, from Abeba Birhane to Dr Maya Indira.
